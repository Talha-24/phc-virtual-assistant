What is Pond Hockey Club?
Pond Hockey Club (PHC) is an elite hockey training facility for kids and teens, focused on building tomorrow's stars through comprehensive development in hockey skills, education, health, and character.

Where is Pond Hockey Club located?
Pond Hockey Club is located at 555 Pleasantville Rd STE 200, Briarcliff Manor, NY 10510.

The website is https://pond-hockey-club.vercel.app/

What is the philosophy behind Pond Hockey Club?
Pond Hockey Club believes in maximizing repetitions and building grit through consistent practice. They leverage an always-available synthetic ice surface to transform limited training time into real, measurable development, following the principle that players should maximize practice time rather than waiting for limited ice availability.

What is the John Mariucci quote that represents the club's philosophy?
"There are too many one-hour players. They're waiting for one hour of indoor ice when they could use the natural ice God has given them for five or six hours." — John Mariucci, Minnesota Gopher Player & Coach

What is the mission of Pond Hockey Club?
The mission is to develop elite hockey skills while building resilience, character, and a genuine love for the game. They focus on creating well-rounded athletes who understand that greatness comes through dedication, repetition, and mental toughness.

What are the core values of Pond Hockey Club?
The four core values are: Hockey (elite skill development), Education (learning and mental development), Health (physical fitness and well-being), and Character (building resilience, discipline, and mental toughness).

How many players are in each training group?
Training groups are kept small, typically 4-6 players per session, to ensure personalized attention and maximum ice touches for every athlete.

What type of ice surface does Pond Hockey Club use?
Pond Hockey Club uses a synthetic ice surface that is always available for training, not dependent on traditional ice time schedules.

What is the training approach at Pond Hockey Club?
The training approach centers on high-repetition training with structured drills, game-like scenarios, and continuous feedback. Sessions are designed to push players beyond their comfort zones while building confidence one repetition at a time.
